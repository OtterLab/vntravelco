Welcome to Vietnam

Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon) is the busiest, dizzying, cultural and the largest city in Vietnam. The city locates South of Vietnam region along the Saigon River and Mekong River Delta, which is about 50 miles (80km) from the South China Sea. Ho Chi Minh City is a popular tourists and business destination based on the classic French architecture, Asian culture and beautiful skyscrapers including temples and pagodas. Other most popular city destinations such as Nha Trang, Dalat, Danang, Hanoi, Ha Long Bay, Phu Quoc Island and Sapa.

Top Travel Tips

It is important to have the right travel documents before you go. To enter the country, it is required to have a valid visa entry and must hold a valid passport.

Be wary of taxis and motorcycle scams. Scams can happen, and your best way to avoid transport scams is to know where you are going along the way and ask the driver about travel distance and quote a price before travelling. The two major taxi companies are Vinasun and Mai Linh (both have apps) in Vietnam. Also, other transport companies like Grab and Uber (both have apps) are valuable options. Driver’s will call you to confirm, so having a Vietnamese speaking friend or family member nearby will be handy.

Tourists enjoys delicious food and want to try it all. Be beware of how the food was made and hygiene when you’re eating street food. Always wash and peel your fruits and vegetables before eating. Also, avoiding drinking tap water.

Vietnam is hot and humid. The Northern Vietnam is hot wet summers and cool dry winters up North. It may be cold in the northern hemisphere in winter from September to November. The Central Vietnam experiences hot, dry weather between January and August when temperatures hit 30 degrees and above. The Southern Vietnam is full of tropical climate with 2 seasons – wet and dry. The best time to visit are during December to April.

Keeping yourself and others safe is important. Crime and violent is rare including other cities. Beware of your personal surroundings, always travel with group of people. When crossing the streets, motorbikes are trying to anticipate your movements to avoid hitting you. Walking slow and steady to cross the road. Never carry or hold any items for another person. Always keep your valuable items such as wallets, mobile phones, cameras and other electronic devices safe. Carry a purse that has a strap that goes across your body and keep it in front of you. Never carry your passport in your backpack. Leave it at a hotel with a reception, lock cupboard or with a family member. Many retail shopping centres accept visa and credit cards from today. Still they used cash to pay mostly everything. Always use cash which minimise the risks of fraud and scam.

Sightseeing and Landmarks

Sun World Bana Hills

Bana Hills is one of the popular tourist’s attraction in Vietnam. Its known for its mountaintop resort that looks like a medieval castle and French style architecture design. There are lots of rides, sightseeing, restaurants and hotels. Also, the iconic Golden Bridge at Bana Hills.

Reunification Palace (Dinh Độc Lập)

The Reunification Place is a tourist attraction. It is located at Man Ky Khoi Nghia Street, close to the Ben Thanh Market. Today its a must visit attraction in Ho Chi Minh City. Outside the palace you can see two tanks located at the park. The palace was home and workplace of French Governor of Cochin_China. It has a lush gardens, antique furniture, secret rooms and a commander bunker.

Dalat Flower Park

The flower park features a variety of different flower gardens. It is located on the north shore around Xuan Huong Lake, on the valley of Cu Hill. The park consists of at least 300 different kinds of flowers and blossoms. Also, it features a small cafe on site. This is a must see in Dalat.

Shop & Dine

Estella Place

Estella Place shopping centre is located in District 2, Song Hanh, An Phu. There are many retail stores and restaurants to choose from such as cafes, multicultural cuisines, fashion clothing, grocery stores, home decors and more.

Vincom Landmark 81

Landmark 81 is the second tallest skyscraper. The tower offering a large selection of restaurants, shops, cinema, ice rink, hotel and work facilities.

Bánh Cuốn Thiên Hương

Rice Rolls filled with Pork and Mushrooms (Banh Cuon) is avietnamese steamed rice rolls is a traditional dish. The rice rolls serve with fresh green mints, bean sprouts and prawn cakes.

Du Mien Garden Cafe

Du Mien cafe is a outdoor cafe located in Go Vap. The venue has lots of trees, ponds, waterfalls, building up a slight hill-country Da Lat type. This can be a great place to enjoy delicious food and a refreshing drink.
